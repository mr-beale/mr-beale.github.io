The Audience

General Audience

Generally, this website is intended for people in Southern Idaho who need to know about local weather conditions and events to plan their activities. This is a very broad audience that includes people of all ages, incomes, and technology.

Persona 1 - The Gardener

The home hobbiest gardener.Interested in current weather forcasts to plan the propagation and care of their garden. Information about events such as local farmers markets are also of prime interest. Most likely to visit the site using a cell phone or laptop

Persona 2 - The Event Planner

This group includes a wide range of people from Mom's planning a child's birthday party to couples planning a wedding party. Outdoor weather conditions can make or break a good plan. Knowing what events are around may spark an idea for a venue or let you know what areas to avoid. Are there other big events happening on the same day? Most likely to check the website using a mobile device like a cell phone or tablet. Some traffic may come from laptop/desktop computers.

User Scenarios

Scenario 1:

Bobb is planting a garden. A particular plant is very sensitive to cold temperatures. She needs to know if what day this week will have the best conditions for planting.

Scenario 2:

Joe has a large crop of raspberries ready to pick. He wants to know when the next Farmer's market in Preston will be held so he can take some of his berries to sell. Once he learns what the date is, he is curious about the weather so he can plan what equipment he should take for his booth to proct his berries and provide a comfortable environment for potential customers.

Scenario 3:

Mary is planning her wedding party. She want's to host it outside but needs to know if weather will interfear with her plans. She also wants to check local events to make sure that there are no conflicts that would tie up her preffered venue or prevent her guests from attending her party.

Scenario 4:

Ryan works in a company's HR department. He's been tasked with planning a company luncheon for the employees. He looks at the weather forcast to pick the best date for the event. He uses the list of current events to narrow down possible venues for the luncheon.

Scenario 5:

The Smith family is planning their spring break. They want to go camping at one of the local campsites. They check the weather forcast so they know what kinds of gear they need to pack for their camping trip. They can check campsite availability in the local events area of the site.
